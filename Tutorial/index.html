- Unconscious Bias -

When you mention unconscious bias, people tend to get defensive, or dismissive. So let's begin by taking a step back, and looking at the larger picture...

Next

As humans, our brains are wired for pattern-recognition and narrative building. We are community-based creatures shaped heavily by social influences.

Throughout our lives, we absorb signals from our culture about what's good and bad, often without realizing that we are learning or internalizing these values.

The idea behind unconscious bias is that it affects us in ways we're not aware of.

Naturally, we are influenced by our own experiences. Less so by other people's. This can make us less thoughtful about the opinions we accept about others.

No one is immune to this.
People who have experienced bias can still be biased towards others.

Okay, that makes sense. So what can I do?

What You Can Do

  • Be aware of your own biases. Evaluate your own assessments.
  • Break the in-group/out-group mentality.
    Expand notions of the "right fit" for the "company culture."
  • Actively seek diversity to counteract bias against it.
  • Actively work to retain and support recruits from diverse backgrounds.
  • SPEAK OUT - in private settings when you hear others
    reinforcing harmful assumptions.
  • LISTEN - to the experiences of others.
